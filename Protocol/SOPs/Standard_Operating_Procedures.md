## 3. Clinical Examination SOP

### 3.1 Equipment Preparation
**Purpose:** Ensure equipment readiness and calibration

**Daily Checklist:**
- [ ] Blood pressure apparatus calibrated
- [ ] Stethoscope functional
- [ ] Peak flow meters calibrated
- [ ] Skin prick test solutions prepared
- [ ] Emergency medications available
- [ ] Gloves, masks, and PPE stocked

### 3.2 Examination Protocol
**Purpose:** Standardized clinical assessment

**Sequence:**
1. **Vital Signs (5 minutes):**
   - Blood pressure (3 readings, average)
   - Pulse rate and rhythm
   - Respiratory rate
   - Temperature
   - Oxygen saturation

2. **General Examination (5 minutes):**
   - Height and weight measurement
   - BMI calculation
   - General appearance assessment

3. **Respiratory Examination (10 minutes):**
   - Inspection, palpation, percussion, auscultation
   - Peak flow measurement (3 attempts, best recorded)

4. **ENT Examination (5 minutes):**
   - Nose, throat, and ear assessment

5. **Skin Examination (5 minutes):**
   - General skin assessment
   - Specific allergic conditions

### 3.3 Skin Prick Testing Protocol
**Purpose:** Standardized allergen testing

**Preparation:**
- Clean forearm with alcohol
- Mark test sites (2 cm apart)
- Prepare allergen extracts and controls

**Testing Procedure:**
1. Apply drop of allergen extract
2. Prick through drop with sterile lancet
3. Wipe excess solution
4. Time reactions at 15-20 minutes
5. Measure wheal and flare diameters

**Safety Measures:**
- Emergency medications ready
- Trained personnel only
- Participant monitoring for 30 minutes post-test

### 3.4 Emergency Response
**Purpose:** Handle adverse events during examination

**Protocol:**
1. Stop procedure immediately
2. Assess severity of reaction
3. Administer appropriate treatment
4. Call emergency services if needed
5. Document incident thoroughly
6. Report to principal investigator within 24 hours
